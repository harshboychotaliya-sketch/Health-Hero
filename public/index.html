Plain Language Diagnosis

What You Have

You have been diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. This means your body isn't using insulin properly, so your blood sugar stays higher than normal. This is very manageable — with medicines, the right diet, and light exercise, most people live completely normal lives.

Original (Jargon)
T2DM with peripheral neuropathy and mild nephropathy. HbA1c 8.4%, eGFR 62.
Plain Language
Type 2 Diabetes with mild nerve tingling in feet and slight kidney strain. Blood sugar needs better control.

Side Effect Alerts

Watch For
  • ⚠️

    Nausea or Upset Stomach

    Metformin can cause nausea early on — always take it after food. Usually settles in 1–2 weeks.

  • 🚨

    Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia)

    Sudden sweating, trembling, confusion or dizziness — eat something sweet immediately and call your doctor.

  • ⚠️

    Swelling or Dark Urine

    Due to mild kidney strain — watch for puffiness or very dark urine. Contact your doctor if this occurs.

Follow-Up Checklist

To Do
  • 🩸 Blood test — HbA1c & KFT in 2 weeks
  • 🩺 Follow-up visit with your doctor — 14 days from today
  • 🍽️ Avoid sugar, white rice, fried food. Eat more vegetables & whole grains.
  • 🚶 Walk 20–30 minutes every day. No heavy exercise for now.
  • 👟 Check feet daily for any sores or numbness.
